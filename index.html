Entrepreneurs Only

  • Learn more about HN (unofficially) here.

  • Launched in 2007. Social news site catered specifically to programmers and entrepreneurs.

  • Created by Paul Graham, co-founder of Y Combinator, a seed accelerator that invests mostly in tech start-ups.

  • Many topics and discussions focus on increasing ones entrepreneurial chops.

  • In fact, founders of many well-known start-ups are active users. [matasano: tptacek, 4chan: moot].




Cognitive Surplus

  • Many posts are purely the result of cognitive surplus.

  • Moderators ensure that posts are mostly productive. But, addiction to social applications is still a problem.

  • "I've learned a lot from things I've read on HN. I've written several essays that began as comments there. So I wouldn't want the site to go away. But I would like to be sure it's not a net drag on productivity. What a disaster that would be, to attract thousands of smart people to a site that caused them to waste lots of time. I wish I could be 100% sure that's not a description of HN."
     -Paul Graham, founder of HN and Y Combinator.

The Future Internet

  • Emphasis on collaboration. Particularly with Show HN, Ask HN and Who's hiring posts.

  • Aggregated lists of Show HN posts here.

Collaborative Tools

  • Many HN users are founders of companies or contractors who rely on remote work.
    There is much discussion on effective and efficient collaborative tools.

  • While HN hires moderators and they collaborate to keep content reasonable and on-topic,
    the community also self-moderates. Users collaborate

  • Users can upvote (and downvote, after a certain karma threshold) posts and comments.

Future of Work

  • Open office layouts are a hot topic on HN.

  • Since many users work remotely or do contract work,
    colocating offices are another popuplar topic.

Privacy & Anonymity

  • Provides usernames with no requirement of self-identifying.

  • However, the faces behind many popular accounts are known.

Friends

  • HN's growth has shifted the 'feel' of the community due to increased amounts of users.

  • It seems that certain posts attract partiuclar users.

  • Y Combinator fosters a tight knit community of digital natives.

PEW Research

  • Studies are often submitted to HN.
